Fishing Gear

Rod & Reel

A rod and a reel are essential to the recreational fisherman. The rod is cast, hold and support the fishing line. The rod is what gives a fisher leverage over a fish. The reel is used to hold the line and spool it up after it has been cast out.

Tackle Box

The Tackle Box is used to store all of a fishers odds and ends of fishing. These may include lures, sinkers, extra line, or even hooks.

Fishing Line

The line is used to attach the bait/lure to the reel. Lines come in different weight ratings so a fisher can optimize their fishing. The skinner and less weight rating, the less visible the line is in the water.

Hardbaits

These lures are usually used to catch more aggressive predatory fish. These fish types may include but are not limited to Walleye, Bass, Pike, Perch, or even Trout

Rubber Worms

These lures are usually used to catch most pan fish. The fish that may be caugh with rubber worms may include but are not limited to bluegill, perch, sunfish, crappie, and sometime bass.
